Pet First Aid

Learn how to administer basic first aid to your pet in case of emergencies. This information is suitable for all types of pets.

Basic First Aid Steps

  • Assess the Situation: Check for safety. Ensure both you and your pet are in a safe environment.
  • Stay Calm: Your pet can sense your emotions. Staying calm will help them feel secure.
  • Control Bleeding: Apply pressure to the wound using a clean cloth. If bleeding persists, seek veterinary help.
  • Check for Breathing: If your pet is not breathing, perform CPR. For small pets, place them on their side and give gentle breaths.
  • Choking: If your pet is choking, perform the Heimlich maneuver. For small pets, hold them with their back against your chest and give a few thrusts.
  • Burns: For minor burns, cool the area with running water. Never use ice. Cover with a clean cloth and seek veterinary help.
  • Poisoning: If you suspect poisoning, contact your veterinarian immediately. Bring the product label with you if possible.

Specific Situations

  • Heatstroke: Move your pet to a cooler area, offer water, and apply cool (not cold) water to their body. Seek veterinary assistance.
  • Fractures: Immobilize the injured area and avoid moving your pet unless necessary. Seek immediate veterinary care.
  • Seizures: Clear the area around your pet, and do not try to restrain them. After the seizure, comfort your pet and consult a veterinarian.
  • Diarrhea or Vomiting: Monitor your pet. Withhold food for 12 hours but provide water. If symptoms persist, consult your veterinarian.

Important Tips

Always keep a pet first aid kit handy, and familiarize yourself with the contents. Regularly check and update the kit as needed. Include items such as:

  • Gauze and bandages
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Thermometer
  • Pet-safe pain reliever (consult your vet)
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Emergency contact numbers for your veterinarian
